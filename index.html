PRIVACY POLICY

Our product is called [insert name here]. It is a mental health app which aims to better the mental health of youth. We allow youth to listen to podcasts and interact with presenters and fellow listeners through posting jamboard style comments. These comments will be heavily vetted to foster a positive community. As such, some information will be collected.


The App will collect a minimal amount of information. Users’ analytic data will be collected to help improve our performance. This information will include how long they use the app, the times of day they use the app, and information collected from a satisfaction survey. Please note that this information will be anonymized. Any individual who has access to these analytics will only be able to see the general statistical information. This information will be collected to help us improve the app for other users.


Data will be collected through usage logs within the app. If you agree to this policy, we won’t ask for consent every time we collect information however you are free to rescind consent at any time. Before choosing to delete the app, you will have the option to remove your information from our database. If you delete the app before doing so, there will unfortunately be no way to remove your information.


Data will not be shared directly with third parties for commercial purposes. This data may be included as part of an academic study which could be published. At this point, it would be available for public use however your individual data would be part of the consolidated data; thus, it would be impossible to trace it to you.


To ensure the security of the personal information, we have hired technical experts to optimize our safety systems. While we will make every effort to protect your data, it is impossible to provide a 100% safety guarantee.


This privacy policy is effective as of August 11 2020. It is, however, subject to change. If you have the app still downloaded, we will notify you within the app of the change. If you have deleted the app, there will unfortunately be no way to contact you to notify you of this change. We will, however, publish any changes on our website by updating this policy.

DESCRIPTION IDEA

Here is a brief overview of our product.


  • What the Product Does

  • Our product is an anonymous social platform that tackles the different problems that kids 11 to 13 could be dealing with. As a Podcast app it allows them to get a live response to each story.

  • Current Gaps in Research

  • Not many apps today have live feedback where the person reading the child’s story can respond, or the child listening to a story can relate. Other apps give tips and advice on how to cope with the problems they might deal with. This app addresses all problems that a child 11 to 13 might be dealing with.

  • How it Works

  • We have designed an app that allows kids with any sort of problems they might have to listen to fictional and non-fictional stories, these stories can relate to all sorts of real life scenarios where they might need help. This will allow them to find where the problem is and cope and or fix it.

  • Target Audience

  • Our app is more targeted towards kids from the age 11 to 13 as that’s where they start to use phones and download more apps, that’s also the age where they start to grow up and realize changes being made in their life and might need help understanding what solutions they need to their problems.

  • Relevant Stakeholders

  • This addresses the needs as it allows kids to have a further understanding of mental health and everyday problems that may occur in their day to day lives.

MARKETING STRATEGY

The app is completely free to download, and we do not sell our users’ data. We make money on a freemium business model. Free users are welcome to access most of the podcasts but will have audio ads (visual ads will take away from the user interface). Premium users will pay $1.99 per month for the ability to download podcasts, have access to all of the podcasts, and have no ads.


Social media marketing and word-of-mouth marketing are likely to be the two most important facets of the marketing plan. Social media is cheap to use and appeals to the parents of our ultimate consumers. We will be aided by both organic word-of-mouth marketing (authentic, traditional word-of-mouth marketing) and amplified word-of-mouth marketing. Our amplified word of marketing plan is a referral system where every referral unlocks exclusive podcasts for free users and discounts for premium users. This will accelerate word-of-mouth marketing. Word-of-mouth is appropriate for our app because it is completely free, and suggestions from friends and family are seen as more genuine.


Most importantly, not all children have access to their personal computer or a smartphone; we must get the parents involved in order to let these potential users get involved. Instead of ads, parents trust apps which the teachers and the schools recommend. We can contact primary school boards and children's mental health NGOs and let them recommend our app to the parents if they believe it is worth downloading.


Users will be randomly asked on occasion to fill in a short quantitative survey. Survey questions will be specific to the visual design of the app and podcast details. These two facets of our team’s vision are key to creating a product tailored to our unique target market. The additional exclusive podcasts or discounts on subscription will also be used as incentive for completing feedback surveys.


The nature of our “post-it” comment box on each podcast episode allows for a seamless way for users to submit suggestions. Our team views, considers, and responds to each and every suggestion. Not only does this allow for our podcasts to be better in the future and our app to have new and improved updates, but it lets the users know that their opinions are valued.
